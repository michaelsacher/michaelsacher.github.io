Vex Robotics

Competition Based Robotics Project

Overview

Competed in VEX Robotics for 2 seasons, earning ~15 awards including an international signature event win at Sugar Rush and qualifying for the World Championship. Designed and built ~15 robots using Autodesk Inventor and Fusion, while leading my team as programmer, strategist, designer, and driver during the first season. Combined leadership with advanced technical skills by developing state-level competitive robots, creating innovative autonomous strategies, and applying advanced motion algorithms in C++ to consistently achieve high performance.

Project Videos

Technical Details

Robots Designed: ~15 across 2 seasons, each applying unique drive bases and scoring mechanisms

Programming: C++ with PROS, implementing custom motion algorithms (PID, Pure Pursuit, Ramsete, Monte Carlo Localization, position resets, and path smoothing)

CAD Tools: Autodesk Inventor & Fusion for pre-build analysis and weakpoint identification

Prototyping: Utilized additive manufacturing (3D printing) for rapid iteration and plastic optimization for weight reduction

Design Approach: Engineering design process reinforced by scouting high-level competitions and open collaboration with advanced teams

Design Process

Followed the engineering design process from brainstorming through prototyping, applying CAD to identify structural weaknesses before building. During assembly, iterative adjustments were made to resolve overlooked issues. Actively researched high-level competitions and engaged with top-tier teams to refine strategies, while integrating consistent communication within the team to adapt designs quickly. Each subsystem (drive, intake, scoring) was tested and refined for reliability under competition conditions.

Key Achievements

  • Earned ~15 awards in 2 seasons, including an international signature event win at Sugar Rush
  • Developed one of the first fully solo Autonomous Win Point routines, boosting competitive rankings significantly
  • Led team to the State Championship in first season while balancing roles as programmer, strategist, builder, and driver
  • Qualified for the World Championship with consistent autonomous and advanced motion planning algorithms

Technologies Used

PROS (C++) Autodesk Inventor Fusion 360 Advanced Motion Algorithms Engineering Design Process Additive Manufacturing

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: In the first season, managing time across programming, building, driving, and team leadership often left little time for testing and refining code.

Solution: Prioritized scheduling and streamlined build processes to create more dedicated programming time, allowing for robust autonomous development.

Challenge: In the second season, autonomous consistency was hindered by odometry drift with an all-Omni wheel drivetrain.

Solution: Implemented path smoothing and more rugged odometry handling in code to increase reliability, which became a cornerstone of competitive performance.

Future Development

Skills gained from VEX Robotics — including CAD, motion algorithm development, and iterative engineering — are being applied to larger-scale projects such as aerospace prototypes and robotics research. Future projects will expand these experiences by incorporating advanced control systems, data-driven optimization, and cross-disciplinary engineering approaches.
